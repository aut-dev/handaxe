# Character Design Sheet: CAPTAIN LINDA

## A Day in the Life of Todd — Handaxe Studios

---

## Identity

- **Full name:** Linda Vasquez
- **Age:** 44
- **Rank:** Captain, City Fire Department, Engine Company 19
- **Background:** Twenty-year veteran. Grew up in the neighborhood Todd moved into. Has seen everything. Divorced, no kids (never stated, just implied by the way she has time to stop by off-duty). She is the most competent person in Todd's world and the only person in his life who isn't pretending
- **Demeanor:** Direct, patient, dry. She doesn't sugarcoat, but she doesn't mock. She treats Todd the way a good teacher treats a struggling student — with clarity and the quiet expectation that he'll figure it out

---

## First Appearance

**Episode 4: "Grilled Cheese"** — on-duty, in the background of Todd's shot after his pan fire. Arms crossed. Unimpressed but not unkind.

**NOTE:** Linda does not appear in Episode 1. This sheet exists to lock her design before production reaches Ep 4, and to ensure any series-level reference materials are consistent.

---

## Face

- **Shape:** Square-ish, strong. A face built for authority. High cheekbones, defined jaw
- **Eyes:** Dark brown, nearly black. Steady. She holds eye contact longer than most people are comfortable with. When she's amused (which Todd causes frequently), her eyes narrow and the corners crinkle before she allows a smile — if she allows one at all
- **Eyebrows:** Strong, dark, naturally thick. She doesn't raise them for emphasis the way Todd does. When she's skeptical, one eyebrow lifts a fraction of an inch. That's it. That's enough
- **Nose:** Prominent, slightly wide. A nose with character
- **Mouth:** Often set in a neutral line that people misread as stern. Her actual resting expression is calm concentration. When she smiles — rare, earned, usually because of something Todd does — it transforms her face completely. Warm and wide
- **Skin:** Medium-deep brown, warm undertones. Sun-worn around the eyes and forehead (twenty years of responding to outdoor calls). A small scar on her left jawline — old, faded, never explained

---

## Hair

- **Color:** Black with strands of silver at the temples. She doesn't dye it. The silver is a feature, not a flaw
- **Length:** Shoulder-length when down (we see this only in Ep 9, off-duty)
- **On duty:** Pulled back in a tight, low bun. Practical. No flyaways. Everything about on-duty Linda says "controlled"
- **Off duty (Ep 9):** Down, or in a loose ponytail. Softer. The visual difference between on-duty Linda and off-duty Linda should be noticeable — same person, different energy

---

## Body

- **Height:** 5'7" — shorter than Todd, but she doesn't seem shorter. She carries herself with compressed authority
- **Build:** Strong, solid. Not bulky — functionally fit. She looks like someone who carries heavy things for a living because she does. Broad shoulders for her frame. She stands with her weight evenly distributed, feet shoulder-width apart. Fire captain posture
- **Hands:** Working hands. Short nails, strong grip. In Ep 12 when she cooks with Todd, her hands should look capable and practiced — she's not a chef, but she's someone who knows how to handle tools and heat
- **Movement:** Economical. No wasted gestures. She walks into a room and takes a position. She doesn't pace, fidget, or lean

---

## Wardrobe

### On-Duty (Eps 4, 5, 6)
- **Standard fire captain turnout:** Dark navy uniform shirt with department patches, badge on chest, name tape "VASQUEZ." Dark navy pants. Black boots. The uniform should look worn but maintained — not brand-new, not shabby. A uniform that's been through things
- **No helmet in Todd's apartment** — she's already assessed the scene and removed it by the time we see her. She might carry a clipboard (billing paperwork in Ep 6)

### Off-Duty (Eps 9, 12)
- **Episode 9:** Simple civilian clothes. Dark green field jacket over a grey t-shirt. Jeans. Work boots (different from fire boots — broken-in brown leather). She dresses practically. Nothing expensive, nothing curated. She looks like she ran an errand and stopped by
- **Episode 12 (cooking):** Same jacket, or a flannel shirt with sleeves rolled up. She ties a dish towel around her waist as a makeshift apron — a small detail that shows she knows kitchens, just not professionally

### The Contrast with Todd
Linda's wardrobe is the anti-Todd: everything is appropriate to the situation, nothing cost more than sixty dollars, all of it is worn because it works. Standing next to Todd in his cashmere and loafers, she looks like the adult in the room. Because she is.

---

## Expression Sheet

### Key Expressions

**1. The Assessment**
Arms crossed, weight on one hip, chin level. She's evaluating the situation. This is her default in early appearances — the fire captain stance. Not hostile, but absolutely in charge.

**2. The Patience**
A slow blink. A measured exhale through the nose. She's waiting for Todd to finish doing or saying something ridiculous before she responds. She gives him the space to be wrong, then corrects him without fanfare.

**3. The Almost-Smile**
Eyes crinkle. Mouth stays closed but one corner twitches upward. She will not give Todd the satisfaction of laughing at his bits. She is, however, charmed despite herself. This is the expression the audience falls in love with.

**4. The Teacher**
Focused, direct, hands demonstrating. When she shows Todd how to use medium heat, how to spatchcock a chicken — her face is engaged, clear, patient. No condescension. She explains the way someone explains to an adult they respect, not a child they're managing.

**5. The Concern**
Rare. Seen in Ep 9 (the rice failure visit) and Ep 12 (the phone call). Her jaw softens. The authority drops. She looks at Todd like she's seeing the real person under the hosting voice. This expression is quiet and unperformative.

---

## Voice (Audio Reference)

- **Register:** Alto. Clear and carrying — a voice trained to be heard over sirens and chaos
- **Pacing:** Faster than Todd's. Clipped, efficient. She says what she means and stops. No filler words
- **Tone range:** Narrow on-duty (professional, even). Wider off-duty — she has a dry humor that lands in her delivery, not her words. She'll say something completely deadpan that's devastating
- **Key line deliveries:**
  - *"Todd. It's been four days."* — flat, a statement of fact that's also the funniest line in the episode
  - *"You did that. I just told you the temperatures."* — warm, genuine, the most generous thing anyone says to Todd all season

---

## The Linda-Todd Dynamic (Visual Notes)

Their scenes together should feel like **two different shows colliding:**
- Todd is always performing for the camera. Linda never is
- Todd fills the frame loosely, casually. Linda occupies her space with precision
- Todd's body language is open (palms out, gestures wide). Linda's is contained (arms crossed, hands at sides, minimal movement)
- When Linda enters Todd's apartment, the temperature of the shot changes — she brings the cooler outside-world palette into his warm amber cave
- Over the season, this gap narrows. By Ep 12, they're in the same color temperature. They're in the same show

## Character Consistency Notes for AI Generation

**Anchoring features (must be consistent):**
- The scar on the left jawline
- Silver at the temples
- Strong, level posture — feet apart, weight even
- Dark brown eyes, steady gaze

**On-duty vs. off-duty visual shift:**
- On-duty: hair up, uniform, contained movement, cooler lighting association
- Off-duty: hair down or loose, civilian clothes, slightly more relaxed stance, warmer tones
