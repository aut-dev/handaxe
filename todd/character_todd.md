# Character Design Sheet: TODD

## A Day in the Life of Todd — Handaxe Studios

---

## Identity

- **Full name:** Todd Aldrich (surname never spoken on the show; appears only on the legal document in Ep 13)
- **Age:** 31
- **Background:** Heir to a wealthy family. Walked away three months before the series begins. No job, no friends, no life skills. Moved into a studio apartment with a suitcase of expensive clothes and nothing else
- **Demeanor:** Earnest, unhurried, warm. He speaks to an audience of three like he's addressing millions. He is never performing irony. He is always performing sincerity

---

## Face

- **Shape:** Soft oval. Not angular, not round. A face that reads "approachable" before anything else
- **Eyes:** Brown, slightly large for his face. Expressive — they do more work than his mouth. When he's thinking, his eyes drift up and to the left. When he's proud, they widen. When he's pretending something tastes good, they narrow just slightly
- **Eyebrows:** Medium thickness, naturally arched. They lift constantly — surprise, emphasis, encouragement. Todd's eyebrows are always inviting you to be as excited as he is
- **Nose:** Straight, unremarkable. Slightly upturned at the tip
- **Mouth:** Medium-full lips. His resting expression is a very slight closed-mouth smile — not smug, just default-pleasant. When he's genuinely delighted (rare but real), his whole mouth opens and his teeth show and it's impossible not to like him
- **Jaw:** Soft. Not weak, but not chiseled. He has never once thought about his jawline
- **Stubble:** 2-3 days of growth, always. Not a beard, not clean-shaven. He doesn't own a razor — or if he does, he forgets. Light brown, slightly patchy near the sideburns
- **Skin:** Fair with warm undertones. A few freckles across the bridge of his nose. No tan. This is a man who has been indoors for three months. Slight under-eye shadows by mid-season, but in Ep 1, he looks rested — he's still new at this

---

## Hair

- **Color:** Medium brown with the faintest warm highlights (the kind expensive shampoo used to maintain; now fading)
- **Length:** Too long for a style, too short for a look. Hits just above his ears on the sides, falls across his forehead. The back touches his collar
- **Texture:** Slightly wavy when it gets in his eyes. Otherwise soft and fine
- **Style:** None. He pushes it to the side with his fingers. It falls back. He pushes it again. This is a recurring micro-gesture — hand through hair, hair falls, repeat
- **Over the season:** Gets progressively longer. By Ep 13, it's noticeably shaggier than Ep 1. He never gets a haircut. He may not know where to get one

---

## Body

- **Height:** 5'11" — average enough to not be notable
- **Build:** Slim-soft. Not fit, not overweight. The body of someone who was probably maintained by a personal trainer and has spent three months eating beans. Narrow shoulders, no visible muscle definition, but not frail. He moves with a kind of oblivious ease — he takes up space without realizing it, the way people who grew up in large houses do
- **Posture:** Good. Unexpectedly good. Straight back, shoulders back. This is old money posture, trained in, impossible to lose. It's one of the subtle tells that something doesn't add up — a guy who can't cook beans standing like he's waiting for a portrait to be painted
- **Hands:** Long fingers. Clean nails (he's fastidious about this even when everything else is falling apart). He gestures when he talks — open palms, pointing at things, holding up ingredients for the camera. His hands are always in frame

---

## Wardrobe

### The Philosophy
Todd wears what he packed. Everything is expensive, nothing is appropriate. He doesn't own casual clothes because he's never had to. He doesn't buy new things because he doesn't know where to shop (or can't afford to after Ep 7). The clothes degrade over the season — stains accumulate, a cuff unravels, the cashmere pills. He doesn't notice.

### Episode 1 Outfit
- **Top:** Navy cashmere crewneck sweater. Slightly too large — it was probably fitted once, but he's lost weight (beans diet). The sleeves are long enough that he pushes them up to his elbows when cooking. A small moth hole near the hem, visible only in close-ups. No undershirt
- **Bottom:** Medium grey wool trousers. Flat front. Tailored but now sitting slightly low — no belt. He's rolled the cuffs once at the ankle, inexpertly. There's a small bean-juice stain on the left thigh by episode's end
- **Shoes:** Italian leather loafers, cognac brown, $800. Worn without socks. Worn in the kitchen. Worn everywhere. These are the only shoes he owns. By mid-season they'll have burn marks and food stains. In Ep 1, they still look mostly respectable
- **No apron.** He doesn't own one. Doesn't occur to him
- **No watch.** (He owns an expensive one, but it's in the junk drawer. He doesn't wear it. Whether this is a choice or an oversight is never stated)

### Season Wardrobe Arc (Reference)
| Episodes | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| 1-3 | Clothes are rumpled but intact. Cashmere sweater is the primary top |
| 4-6 | Visible stains appear. A different sweater (charcoal, also cashmere) rotates in. Pants get a grease spot |
| 7 | "Sold some of my old junk" — the wardrobe may subtly thin out. He wears the same two sweaters more frequently |
| 8-10 | Clothes show real wear. A hole in the elbow. Rolled sleeves become permanent. The loafers are scuffed |
| 11-13 | For the dinner party, he tries: he's wearing the navy cashmere (freshly washed, or at least sponged). It's his best effort. Charlotte and Miles notice it's the same sweater |

## Expression Sheet

### Key Expressions (for AI consistency reference)

**1. The Host**
Todd's default on-camera mode. Slight eyebrow raise, small confident smile, chin slightly up. He looks directly at the lens. This is his Food Network face. He holds it even when things are going wrong.

**2. The Inspection**
Examining an ingredient or result. Brow slightly furrowed, head tilted 5 degrees, lips pressed together. Serious but not grim. He looks like a jeweler appraising a stone. Used for: the furry cheese, the scorched beans, any cooking result.

**3. The Pivot**
When something goes wrong and he's recalculating. Eyes widen briefly, then narrow. Mouth opens, closes, opens again. A micro-nod to himself. Then the body language resets and he moves forward. Used for: the pasta down the drain, the pan too hot, any disaster.

**4. The Aside**
When he breaks from his hosting cadence and says something real. The smile drops. His voice gets quieter. His eyes are softer. He's not performing — he's just talking. This expression should feel noticeably different from the Host. Used for: "I just moved here about three months ago."

**5. The Pride**
Genuine, unguarded. Eyes wide, mouth open, slight lean forward. This is rare in early episodes (just the can-opening victory in Ep 1), but becomes the emotional spine of the show. Used for: opening the can, the rice in Ep 10, the chicken in Ep 12.

**6. The Chew**
Todd eating bad food and deciding it's good. Jaw working, eyes looking upward and to the right (processing), then a definitive nod. The verdict face. "Not bad. Solid seven out of ten."

---

## Voice (Audio Reference)

- **Register:** Baritone-light. Warm, not deep. The kind of voice that sounds like it's smiling even when the face isn't
- **Pacing:** Unhurried. He pauses between thoughts. He is never breathless, never rushed. The pauses are not awkward to him — they're only awkward to us
- **Hosting voice vs. real voice:** The hosting voice is projected, cadenced, slightly louder. It sounds rehearsed even though it isn't. His real voice (heard in Eps 9, 12, 13) is quieter, lower, with more air in it. The transition between these two voices is the show's most important audio cue
- **Signature phrases:** "As you can see," "For those of you following along at home," "Not bad"

---

## Character Consistency Notes for AI Generation

**Anchoring features (must be consistent across every shot):**
- The navy cashmere sweater (Ep 1) — color, texture, fit
- Hair length and parting
- Stubble density
- Posture (straight back)
- Eyebrow expressiveness

**Common drift risks:**
- Face aging/de-aging between shots (lock age at 31, maintain under-eye consistency)
- Sweater color shifting (navy, not black, not dark blue — specifically dark navy with visible knit texture)
- Hair getting shorter between shots (it should always read "slightly too long")
- Build changing (slim-soft, not athletic, not heavy)

**Reference prompt fragment (for generation):**
> 31-year-old man, medium brown slightly wavy hair falling across forehead, light stubble, warm brown eyes, soft oval face with freckles on nose bridge, wearing an oversized navy cashmere crewneck sweater with sleeves pushed to elbows and grey wool trousers, cognac leather loafers, standing in a small apartment kitchen, warm ambient lighting, semi-stylized painterly realism
