### SCENE 1: THE INTRODUCTION

**INT. TODD'S APARTMENT — KITCHEN — DAY**

*The phone is propped against something on the counter — a cereal box, maybe, or a stack of cans. The angle is slightly too low, catching too much ceiling. Todd steps into frame from the side. He adjusts the phone. The frame lurches. Now we see him from the chest up, slightly off-center. Behind him: a bare apartment kitchen. Clean but sparse. A knife block. An empty fruit bowl. A fridge humming audibly.*

*Todd looks at the camera. He takes a breath — the breath of a man about to host a show. He has no audience. He has the cadence of one.*

**TODD:**
Hey everyone. Welcome to A Day in the Life of Todd.

*He gestures around the kitchen like he's showing off a set. There isn't much to show.*

**TODD (CONT'D):**
Today we're going to be cooking — well, let's see what we've got.

*He turns to the fridge. Opens it. The fridge light flickers once, then holds. The interior is almost empty: a can of beans, a block of cheese in plastic wrap, a bottle of something amber and unidentifiable, and not much else.*

*Todd reaches in and pulls out the cheese. He holds it up to the phone. The cheese is visibly furry — a thick coat of blue-green mold. He studies it like a sommelier examining a cork. He turns it in his hand.*

**TODD (CONT'D):**
*(matter-of-fact)*
That's not going to work for us today.

*He places the cheese back in the fridge. Gently. As though it might be useful later.*

*He reaches back in and retrieves the can of beans. Holds it up.*

**TODD (CONT'D):**
Beans. Classic. You really can't go wrong with beans.

*He sets the can on the counter. Looks at it. A moment of quiet confidence.*

---

### SCENE 2: THE CAN

*Todd grips the can. He looks around the counter for a can opener. Opens a drawer — the junk drawer. Rummages. No can opener. He pushes things aside. Closes the drawer.*

*He opens another drawer. Nothing useful. He opens a cabinet. Closes it.*

*He looks at the can. The can looks back.*

*Todd opens the junk drawer again. This time he pulls out a butter knife. He considers it. Then he opens a lower cabinet and pulls out a small hammer — the kind you'd hang a picture with.*

*He sets up: can on the counter, butter knife positioned on the lid like a chisel, hammer in his other hand. He looks at the camera. The gravity in his expression is entirely disproportionate to the task.*

**TODD:**
*(instructional, steady)*
So you want to get a good angle on it. Right on the edge here.

*He taps the butter knife with the hammer. Nothing happens. He taps harder. The knife slips. He repositions. Taps again. A tiny dent in the lid.*

**TODD (CONT'D):**
There we go.

*He works his way around the lid, tapping. It takes a while. It's loud. He narrates like he's filleting a fish.*

**TODD (CONT'D):**
Just working around the perimeter. Nice and even.

*The lid finally pries up — jagged, bent, bean juice on his fingers. He lifts it off with the knife, careful, like he's defusing something. He holds up the open can to the camera. Triumphant.*

**TODD (CONT'D):**
And that's how you do that.

---

### SCENE 3: THE COOKING

*Todd places a pan on the stove. It's a decent pan — looks new, barely used. He dumps the beans in. They sit in the cold pan in a wet lump.*

*He turns the burner to high. All the way. He steps back, satisfied.*

**TODD:**
*(to camera)*
Now I'm going to really crank the heat on this because — and I think a lot of people don't know this — time is flavor. The faster you cook it, the more flavor gets locked in.

*He nods, absolutely certain.*

*He leans against the counter and looks at the camera while the beans heat. For a moment, there's nothing happening. Just Todd, standing in his kitchen, waiting. The fridge hums. A car passes outside. Todd pushes his hair out of his eyes.*

**TODD (CONT'D):**
*(filling the silence)*
So, little bit about me. I just moved here about three months ago. Still getting set up, as you can see.

*He gestures vaguely at the barren apartment behind him. The futon. The two-chair table. The empty walls.*

**TODD (CONT'D):**
But we're making it work. And I figured, you know, I should learn to cook. So that's what this is. This is the journey.

*Behind him, the beans have begun to smoke. A thin wisp, then more. Todd doesn't notice. He's looking at the camera.*

**TODD (CONT'D):**
I actually had this great —

*He stops. Sniffs. Turns around.*

*The beans are scorching. Dark brown crust forming on the bottom, thin smoke rising. The pan is hissing. Todd grabs the handle — no oven mitt — and yanks his hand back.*

**TODD (CONT'D):**
Okay. That's — yep. That's hot.

*He grabs a dish towel, wraps his hand, pulls the pan off the burner. He turns the stove off. Peers into the pan. The beans are half-blackened on the bottom, still cold-looking on top. A culinary crime scene.*

*He looks at the camera. Not defeated. Assessing.*

**TODD (CONT'D):**
*(nodding slowly)*
Okay. Little bit of char on the bottom. That's fine. That's — in a lot of cultures, that's actually preferred. The crispy part.

---

### SCENE 4: THE MEAL

*Todd carries the pan to the sink. He doesn't plate the beans. He stands at the sink and eats them directly from the pan with a fork. The phone is still on the counter, catching him in profile now — he's moved away from his mark, but the camera's still rolling.*

*He chews. The beans are clearly terrible. His expression cycles through something — a micro-wince, then a recovery, then something that might generously be called acceptance.*

*He takes another bite. Chews thoughtfully.*

**TODD:**
*(with the fullest sincerity)*
Not bad. Solid seven out of ten.

*Another bite. He looks out the window above the sink. Late afternoon light. He's alone in the apartment, eating scorched beans from a pan, broadcasting to no one.*

*He doesn't look sad. He looks like a man who made dinner.*

**TODD (CONT'D):**
*(to camera, still chewing)*
Alright. That's going to do it for today. Thanks for watching, everybody. I'll see you next time.

*He sets the pan in the sink. Walks toward the phone. His hand fills the frame as he reaches for it.*

**TODD (O.S.):**
*(quietly, almost to himself)*
Seven out of ten. Maybe a six.

*His thumb covers the lens.*

**CUT TO BLACK.**

### AI Generation Strategy

**Consistency anchors needed:**
- Todd's face/body (every shot)
- The kitchen (background in most shots)
- The cashmere sweater (wardrobe lock)
- The pan, the can of beans, the butter knife (prop continuity)

**Longest continuous generation needed:** Shot 6 (opening the can) at 25s — may need to be split into two 12-13s generations with a cut on Todd's hands repositioning.

**Highest-risk shots:**
- Shot 10 (smoke appearing behind Todd while he talks) — requires environmental change within a single generation
- Shot 12 (Todd eating at sink in profile) — different angle from all other shots; needs character consistency from a new perspective

**Simplest shots (generate first as anchors):**
- Shot 1 (title card — text only)
- Shot 14 (Instagram UI — graphic overlay)
- Shot 2 (Todd standing in kitchen, talking — standard medium shot, no action)
